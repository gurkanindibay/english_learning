## Passage 1: Campus Conversation (~3 minutes)

**Audio File:** `audio/Library_Research_Consultation.mp3`

**Context:** Listen to a conversation between a student and a university librarian.

### Questions - Passage 1

**1. Why does the student go to the library?**
- A) To check out books for a psychology class
- B) To get help finding peer-reviewed articles for a research project
- C) To make a research consultation appointment
- D) To learn how to use Google Scholar

**2. What is the student's research topic?**
- A) The effects of stress on college students
- B) Sleep patterns in young adults
- C) The effects of sleep deprivation on memory and learning
- D) Biological components of learning

**3. According to the librarian, what is an advantage of using library databases instead of Google Scholar?**
- A) Library databases have more articles
- B) Library databases are easier to use
- C) Students can access full-text articles for free through university subscriptions
- D) Google Scholar only has old articles

**4. What does the librarian recommend the student do to find relevant articles? (Choose 2 answers)**
- A) Use the AND operator to combine keywords
- B) Only search for articles from the past year
- C) Use quotation marks for exact phrases
- D) Avoid older foundational studies

**5. How can the student determine if a research paper is influential?**
- A) By checking the length of the article
- B) By looking at how many times it has been cited
- C) By reading the abstract
- D) By checking the author's credentials

**6. What does the librarian say about accessing databases from home?**
- A) It is not possible
- B) Students need special permission
- C) Students can access them using their university login credentials
- D) It only works during library hours

## Passage 2: Academic Lecture (~5 minutes)

**Audio File:** `audio/Sociology_Gentrification.mp3`

**Context:** Listen to part of a lecture in a sociology class. The professor is discussing urban development.

### Questions - Passage 2

**1. What is the lecture mainly about?**
- A) The history of urban development in the United States
- B) The causes, effects, and controversies surrounding gentrification
- C) How to prevent crime in urban neighborhoods
- D) The life of sociologist Ruth Glass

**2. According to the professor, who coined the term "gentrification"?**
- A) A group of urban planners
- B) Artists in London
- C) Ruth Glass, a British sociologist
- D) American real estate developers

**3. What does the professor say about "pioneer gentrifiers"?**
- A) They are usually wealthy investors
- B) They are often young professionals, artists, or students seeking affordable housing
- C) They intentionally try to displace residents
- D) They only move to suburban areas

**4. What is meant by "displacement" in the context of gentrification?**
- A) Moving from cities to suburbs
- B) Original residents being forced to move because they can't afford rising costs
- C) Businesses relocating to better locations
- D) The natural process of urban change

**5. According to the lecture, what are TWO arguments that proponents of gentrification make? (Choose 2 answers)**
- A) It brings investment to neglected areas
- B) It preserves cultural traditions
- C) It can reduce crime rates and improve infrastructure
- D) It creates affordable housing
- E) It prevents urban sprawl

**6. What does the professor say about community land trusts?**
- A) They are owned by the government
- B) Land is owned collectively by a nonprofit and kept permanently affordable
- C) They have been proven ineffective
- D) They only work in rural areas

**7. Listen again to part of the lecture. Then answer the question:**
*"Now, I want to be clear - gentrification isn't inherently good or bad. That's an oversimplification."*

**Why does the professor say this?**
- A) To introduce competing perspectives on a complex issue
- B) To state his personal opinion about gentrification
- C) To confuse students about the topic
- D) To dismiss both sides of the debate

**8. What is the professor's attitude toward gentrification?**
- A) He strongly supports it
- B) He strongly opposes it
- C) He presents it as a complex issue requiring understanding of structural factors
- D) He believes it is not important

**9. According to the professor, what makes addressing gentrification challenging?**
- A) Lack of research on the topic
- B) Solutions require political will and often conflict with powerful economic interests
- C) People don't care about the issue
- D) It only affects a small number of people

**10. What does the professor ask students to do for next class?**
- A) Write an essay on gentrification
- B) Interview residents of gentrified neighborhoods
- C) Read a case study on Brooklyn's gentrification in Chapter 8
- D) Research community land trusts
