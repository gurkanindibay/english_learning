## Passage 1: Campus Conversation (~7 minutes)

**Audio File:** `audio/Research_Ethics_Committee_Discussion.mp3`

**Context:** Listen to a conversation between a professor and a graduate student discussing the student's research proposal.

### Questions - Passage 1

## Passage 2: Academic Lecture (~8 minutes)

**Audio File:** `audio/Art_History_Impressionism_and_Modernism.mp3`

**Context:** Listen to part of a lecture in an art history class. The professor is discussing the transition from Impressionism to Modernism.

### Questions - Passage 2

**1. What is the main topic of the lecture?**
- A) The technical painting methods of Impressionist artists
- B) How Impressionism challenged conventional notions of perception
- C) The biography of Claude Monet
- D) The differences between French and American art movements

**2. According to the professor, what philosophical position did the Impressionists take?**
- A) Painting should represent objective reality
- B) There are only subjective, momentary perceptions
- C) Art should follow academic traditions
- D) Artists should paint primarily indoors

**3. Why does the professor mention Hermann von Helmholtz?**
- A) To show that artists studied his scientific writings
- B) To demonstrate that art and science were reaching similar conclusions about perception
- C) To criticize scientific approaches to understanding art
- D) To prove that Impressionism was based on scientific principles

**4. What does the professor say about C√©zanne's still life paintings?**
- A) They were painted entirely from imagination
- B) They use a single, fixed viewpoint
- C) They synthesize multiple perceptual moments from different angles
- D) They were unsuccessful during his lifetime

**5. The professor pushes back against the "standard art historical narrative." What is her main criticism?**
- A) It oversimplifies modernism as a linear progression toward abstraction
- B) It gives too much credit to European artists
- C) It ignores the influence of photography
- D) It focuses too much on abstract art

**6. According to the professor, what do both Kandinsky and Hopper have in common despite their different styles?**
- A) Both painted realistic scenes
- B) Both created purely abstract works
- C) Both were self-conscious about the medium of painting itself
- D) Both rejected modernist principles

**7. What does the professor suggest Picasso's "Les Demoiselles d'Avignon" challenges?**
- A) The use of oil paint as a medium

- C) The importance of color in painting

- B) What makes something art

**9. Why does the professor say critics had such intense reactions to modernist art?**
- A) The paintings were poorly executed

- A) How to paint in the Impressionist style
- B) Which artist was the most influential
- C) What happens in the postmodern period after modernism's challenges
- D) Why academic painting was superior
