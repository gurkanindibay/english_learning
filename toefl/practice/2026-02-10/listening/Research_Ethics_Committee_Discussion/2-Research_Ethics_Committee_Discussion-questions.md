## Questions

- Passage 1

**1. What is the main purpose of the conversation?**
- A) To discuss whether the student should change his research topic
- B) To address ethical concerns in the student's research proposal
- C) To review the student's data collection results
- D) To prepare the student for a job interview

**2. What is the professor's primary concern about the informed consent procedures?**
- A) The forms are too difficult for parents to understand
- B) Adolescents may not fully comprehend the implications of long-term participation
- C) The study doesn't require parental consent
- D) The consent forms are not available in multiple languages

**3. According to the professor, what problem arises from monitoring participants' social media accounts?**
- A) It violates federal privacy laws
- B) The technology is too expensive
- C) It creates a duty of care if concerning content is discovered
- D) Participants will likely delete their accounts

**4. Why does the professor say self-reported data would be problematic?**
- A) Teenagers are generally dishonest about their social media use
- B) Studies show people significantly underestimate their screen time
- C) It requires too much time from participants
- D) Schools do not allow self-reported research

**5. What solution does the professor suggest for the data collection problem?**
- A) Having parents monitor their children's accounts
- B) Using phone monitoring apps that collect anonymous usage data
- C) Limiting the study to older teenagers only
- D) Conducting interviews instead of digital monitoring

**6. What concern does the professor raise about school-based recruitment?**
- A) Schools typically refuse to participate in research
- B) It creates a subtle coercion problem due to power dynamics
- C) Teachers are not trained to recruit participants
- D) It violates school district policies

**7. What can be inferred about the student's original research design?**
- A) It was completely unethical and must be abandoned
- B) It was innovative but hadn't fully addressed ethical complexities
- C) It had already been approved by the committee
- D) It was identical to Dr. Chen's 2024 study

**8. What does the professor suggest the student add to his research team?**
- A) A licensed counselor
- B) A school administrator
- C) A technology expert
- D) Another graduate student

**9. What is the professor's attitude toward the student's research question?**
- A) Skeptical that it can be answered properly
- B) Critical of its lack of originality
- C) Supportive, noting its importance to the field
- D) Indifferent to its potential contribution

**10. According to the professor, when might the student be able to begin recruitment if revisions are made promptly?**
- A) Immediately after the conversation
- B) Before the end of the current semester
- C) In April, after the March committee meeting
- D) Not until the following academic year
