## Questions

**1. What is the lecture mainly about?**
- A) How to protect coral reefs from tourism
- B) The biology of coral reefs and the threats they face
- C) The history of coral reef research
- D) Different types of marine ecosystems

**2. According to the professor, what is coral?**
- A) A type of colorful plant
- B) A rock formation created by minerals
- C) An invertebrate animal called a polyp
- D) A type of algae

**3. What does the professor say about zooxanthellae?**
- A) They are parasites that harm coral
- B) They are microscopic algae that live symbiotically with coral and provide energy
- C) They are small fish that clean coral
- D) They are bacteria that cause coral disease

**4. Why does the professor compare coral reefs to rainforests?**
- A) Both have similar temperature requirements
- B) Both are found in tropical regions
- C) Both support a disproportionately large percentage of species relative to their size
- D) Both are green in color

- B) They are located in nutrient-rich waters
- C) They efficiently recycle nutrients despite being in nutrient-poor waters
- D) They are all located near the equator
- E) They contain the most species of any ecosystem

**6. What is coral bleaching?**

- B) When coral expels its zooxanthellae due to temperature stress
- C) A cleaning process done by marine biologists
- D) When coral turns white as it grows

**7. According to the professor, what is the effect of ocean acidification on coral?**
- A) It makes coral grow faster
- B) It changes the color of coral to blue
- C) It makes it harder for coral to form and maintain calcium carbonate skeletons

- B) They are necessary but need to be combined with serious climate action
- C) They have already solved the coral reef crisis
- D) They should focus only on tourism restrictions

- A) Ocean temperatures would increase
- B) All marine life would disappear
- C) Coastal communities would be more vulnerable to flooding and storm damage
- D) The ocean would become more acidic

- B) Uncertain but hopeful that tourism will decrease
