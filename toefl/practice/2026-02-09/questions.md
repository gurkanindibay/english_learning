## Passage 1: Campus Conversation (~7 minutes)

**Audio File:** `audio/Academic_Advising_Appointment.mp3`

**Context:** Listen to a conversation between a student and her academic advisor.

### Questions - Passage 1

**1. Why does the student visit the advisor?**
- A) To discuss her poor grades in business classes
- B) To get help choosing elective courses
- C) To discuss changing her major from Business to Environmental Science
- D) To apply for an internship program

**2. What is the student's main concern about switching majors?**
- A) The application process is too competitive
- B) It might take much longer to graduate
- C) Her parents won't approve
- D) Environmental Science is too difficult

**3. According to the advisor, which of the student's completed courses will count toward the Environmental Science major? (Choose 2 answers)**
- A) Chemistry 101
- B) Business Management
- C) Environmental Studies elective
- D) Marketing courses

**4. How many more credits does the student need to complete the Environmental Science major?**
- A) 12 credits
- B) 15 credits
- C) 33 credits
- D) 45 credits

**5. What does the advisor suggest about summer classes?**
- A) They are required for all Environmental Science majors
- B) They could help the student graduate on time
- C) They are cheaper than regular semester courses
- D) They are not available in Environmental Science

**6. According to the advisor, what is an advantage of graduating in December instead of May?**
- A) December graduates get priority in job applications
- B) Many employers prefer January start dates when new budgets begin
- C) December graduation ceremonies are better
- D) There is no advantage mentioned

**7. What does the student need to do to switch to the Environmental Science major?**
- A) Just notify the Registrar's Office
- B) Complete an application with GPA requirements and a statement of purpose

- D) Retake her science courses

**8. What is the acceptance rate for the Environmental Science program?**
- A) 50%
- B) 60%
- C) 70%
- D) 80%

- C) She wants the professor to be her advisor
- D) She needs the professor's permission to change majors

**10. What does the advisor tell the student to do even before being accepted to the Environmental Science program?**
- A) Register for Environmental Science classes
- B) Declare her intent to change majors with the Registrar's Office
- C) Start looking for internships
- D) Meet with Environmental Science faculty

## Passage 2: Academic Lecture (~6 minutes)

**Audio File:** `audio/Psychology_Sleep_and_Memory_Consolidation.mp3`

**Context:** Listen to part of a lecture in a psychology class. The professor is discussing sleep and memory.

### Questions - Passage 2

**1. What is the main topic of the lecture?**
- A) Different stages of sleep
- B) How to improve study habits
- C) The role of sleep in memory consolidation
- D) The causes of sleep disorders

**2. According to the professor, where is information initially stored when you first learn something?**
- A) In the cortex
- B) In the hippocampus
- C) In the frontal lobe
- D) In the neural pathways

**3. What happens during slow-wave sleep?**
- A) The brain deletes unnecessary information
- B) The brain replays neural patterns to strengthen memories
- C) The brain creates new neurons
- D) The brain processes only emotional memories

**4. What did the finger-tapping study demonstrate?**
- A) Practice makes perfect regardless of sleep
- B) Sleep deprivation improves motor skills
- C) The group that slept showed improvement without additional practice
- D) Staying awake is better for learning physical skills

**5. According to the lecture, what type of memory does REM sleep particularly help with?**
- A) Declarative memory and facts
- B) Short-term memory
- C) Procedural memory and emotional processing
- D) Sensory memory

**6. What does the professor say about "memory triage"?**
- A) It's a medical treatment for memory loss
- B) The brain decides what information to keep and what to discard during sleep
- C) It only happens when you're sleep-deprived
- D) It's a study technique for exams

**7. According to the professor, how does the brain determine which information is important to keep? (Choose 3 answers)**
- A) Repetition - information encountered multiple times
- B) Length - longer pieces of information
- C) Emotional significance - strong emotional responses
- D) Novelty - unique or unexpected information
- E) Social context - information learned with others

**8. Why is cramming the night before an exam ineffective for long-term memory?**
- A) You're too tired to focus during the exam
- B) The brain doesn't have time to consolidate information from hippocampus to cortex
- C) You forget to eat properly
- D) Late-night studying damages brain cells

**9. What does the professor say about sleep deprivation and false memories?**
- A) Sleep deprivation prevents all memory formation
- B) Sleep-deprived people are more likely to remember things that didn't happen
- C) Sleep deprivation only affects old memories
- D) False memories help with creativity

**10. What is the "spacing effect" mentioned by the professor?**
- A) Studying in different physical spaces
- B) Spreading study sessions over multiple days with sleep in between
- C) Taking breaks every hour while studying
- D) Spacing out exam dates throughout the semester

**11. What does the professor say about naps?**
- A) They are as effective as nighttime sleep for memory consolidation
- B) A 90-minute nap can include some slow-wave and REM sleep
- C) Short naps are better than long naps for memory
- D) Naps should be avoided before exams

**12. What is "targeted memory reactivation"?**
- A) A technique already widely used in education
- B) Associating information with a cue and presenting that cue during sleep to enhance consolidation
- C) A type of sleep disorder
- D) Reviewing material right before sleep

## Practice Tips

**Key abbreviations to use:**
- w/ = with
- b/c = because  
- ‚Üí = leads to, causes
- ‚Üë = increase
- ‚Üì = decrease
- dept = department
- info = information
- sem = semester
- req = requirement

**Signal words to listen for:**
- **Contrast:** however, but, on the other hand
- **Cause/Effect:** therefore, as a result, because, so
- **Emphasis:** importantly, the key point, remember
- **Examples:** for example, for instance, such as
- **Sequence:** first, second, next, finally
- **Clarification:** in other words, that is, what I mean is
