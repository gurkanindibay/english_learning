## Detailed Strategy by Section

### üéß Listening Strategy (19 ‚Üí 25+)

#### Skill Development Focus
1. **Note-Taking System**
   - Cornell note method
   - Use symbols and abbreviations
   - Capture main ideas vs. details
   - Practice while listening

2. **Signal Words Recognition**
   - Transition markers (however, therefore, in contrast)
   - Emphasis cues (importantly, significantly, the key point)
   - Opinion indicators (I believe, arguably, surprisingly)

3. **Question Type Strategies**
   - Main idea: Focus on intro and conclusion
   - Detail: Note specific examples and facts
   - Function: Why does professor mention X?
   - Attitude: Pay attention to tone and word choice
   - Inference: Combine stated information

#### Practice Materials Needed
- Academic lectures: Science, history, arts, social sciences
- Campus conversations: Student services, academic discussions
- Various accents: American, British, Australian

#### Weekly Milestones
- Week 1-2: Build note-taking system, identify weaknesses
- Week 3-4: Master signal word recognition, improve accuracy 15%
- Week 5-6: Increase speed + accuracy, handle complex lectures
- Week 7-8: Consistent 75%+ accuracy on practice tests

---

### üó£Ô∏è Speaking Strategy (18 ‚Üí 24+)

#### Skill Development Focus
1. **Response Templates** (Critical!)
   - Task 1 (Independent): Opinion template
   - Task 2 (Integrated): Campus situation template  
   - Task 3 (Integrated): Academic concept template
   - Task 4 (Integrated): Academic lecture template

2. **Time Management**
   - Preparation: 15-30 seconds (outline 2-3 key points)
   - Speaking: 45-60 seconds (don't rush, don't run out)
   - Practice starting strong and ending clearly

3. **Delivery Skills**
   - Clear pronunciation of key words
   - Natural pacing (not too fast!)
   - Use filler phrases strategically ("Well," "So," "In other words")
   - Connect ideas with transitions

4. **Content Development**
   - Always include 2-3 specific reasons/examples
   - Use details from reading/listening in integrated tasks
   - Personal examples for independent task

#### Speaking Task Templates

**Task 1 Template (Independent - Opinion)**
```
[Opening] I believe/think that [state opinion clearly].

[Reason 1] First, [main point]. For example, [specific example].

[Reason 2] Additionally, [main point]. [Explanation or example].

[Conclusion] Therefore/So, [restate opinion briefly].
```

**Task 2-4 Templates (Integrated)**
```
[Summary] The reading discusses [topic]. The listening [agrees/disagrees/provides example].

[Point 1] According to the reading, [key point]. The speaker mentions [related listening point].

[Point 2] Furthermore, [second key point from reading]. The lecture explains [supporting detail].

[Conclusion] In summary, [brief wrap-up of relationship].
```

#### Weekly Milestones
- Week 1-2: Master templates, reduce hesitation
- Week 3-4: Fluent delivery, hit time targets consistently
- Week 5-6: Natural-sounding responses, good pronunciation
- Week 7-8: Confident performance under pressure
